## American Civilizations

### Aztecs

#### Jaguar Warrior
- **Age:** Castle Age
- **Cost:** 60 Food, 30 Gold
- **Population:** 1
- **Strong vs:** Infantry, Eagle Warriors
- **Weak to:** Archers, Cavalry
- **Best Use:** Anti-infantry specialist, excellent at countering other infantry units with bonus damage

#### Elite Jaguar Warrior
- **Age:** Imperial Age
- **Cost:** 60 Food, 30 Gold
- **Population:** 1
- **Strong vs:** Infantry, Eagle Warriors, Huskarls
- **Weak to:** Archers, Cavalry
- **Upgrade Benefits:** Enhanced attack bonus vs infantry, improved survivability

---

### Incas

#### Kamayuk
- **Age:** Castle Age
- **Cost:** 60 Food, 30 Gold
- **Population:** 1
- **Strong vs:** Cavalry, Eagle Warriors
- **Weak to:** Archers, Hand Cannoneers
- **Best Use:** Anti-cavalry infantry with range 1, can attack from behind other units
- **Special Ability:** Only melee infantry with 1 range, making them excellent in formations

#### Elite Kamayuk
- **Age:** Imperial Age
- **Cost:** 60 Food, 30 Gold
- **Population:** 1
- **Strong vs:** Cavalry, Eagle Warriors, War Elephants
- **Weak to:** Archers, Hand Cannoneers, Jaguar Warriors
- **Upgrade Benefits:** Increased attack and armor, devastating against cavalry

---

### Mayans

#### Plumed Archer
- **Age:** Castle Age
- **Cost:** 46 Food, 46 Wood
- **Population:** 1
- **Strong vs:** Infantry, Monks
- **Weak to:** Skirmishers, Cavalry
- **Best Use:** Fast-moving archer unit, excellent mobility for hit-and-run tactics
- **Special Ability:** Fastest foot archer in the game

#### Elite Plumed Archer
- **Age:** Imperial Age
- **Cost:** 46 Food, 46 Wood
- **Population:** 1
- **Strong vs:** Infantry, Monks, Siege Weapons
- **Weak to:** Skirmishers, Cavalry, Eagle Warriors

- **Cavalry Masters:** Franks (paladins via bonuses), Persians (War Elephants), Mongols (Mangudai)
- **Archer Civilizations:** Britons (Longbowmen), Mayans (Plumed Archers), Vietnamese (Rattan Archers)
- **Siege Experts:** Celts (siege bonuses), Koreans (War Wagons, Turtle Ships)

## Using This Guide

Each unique unit listing includes:
- **Age of availability** - When you can start producing the unit
- **Resource cost** - Exact food, wood, gold, and stone requirements
- **Population cost** - How much population space the unit takes
- **Counters** - What unit types this unit is strong against
- **Weaknesses** - What counters or threatens this unit
- **Best Use** - Tactical recommendations for optimal deployment
- **Special Abilities** - Unique mechanics or bonuses

---

## Tips for Using Unique Units

1. **Cost Efficiency:** Many unique units are expensive. Consider your economy before massing them.
2. **Synergy:** Combine unique units with your civilization's bonuses for maximum effectiveness.
3. **Counter Play:** Know what counters your unique unit and protect accordingly.
4. **Timing:** Some unique units shine in Castle Age, others need Imperial upgrades.
5. **Trash Units:** Units with no gold cost (Camel Archer, Genoese Crossbowman, etc.) are excellent for late-game.
